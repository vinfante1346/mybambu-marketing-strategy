Risk Matrix

üî¥ HIGH: Platform Readiness

Mitigation: Start platform procurement NOW. Recommend Customer.io or Iterable (faster setup than Salesforce). Allocate 2 weeks for implementation.

üî¥ HIGH: Bonus Budget Overrun

Impact: +$6K unplanned expense

Mitigation: Set hard cap at 1,500 users/month. If demand exceeds, waitlist remaining users. Build $10K contingency into budget.

üî¥ HIGH: Compliance Issues

Risk: Referral program violates state regulations (some states restrict cash incentives)

Impact: Legal penalties, campaign shutdown

Mitigation: Legal review BEFORE launch. Check: NY (strict referral rules), CA (disclosure requirements), TX (generally permissive). Budget $5K for legal counsel.

üü° MEDIUM: Content Quality

Risk: Translated content (not culturally adapted) feels inauthentic to Spanish speakers

Impact: Low engagement, campaign underperforms by 30-40%

Mitigation: Hire native Spanish speaker from target demographic (Mexican, Venezuelan, or Colombian). Have 3 community members review content pre-launch.

üü° MEDIUM: Influencer Misalignment

Risk: Partnered influencers create controversial content, damage brand

Impact: Negative PR, customer backlash

Mitigation: Vet influencers thoroughly (6-month content history review). Include morality clause in contracts. Approve all content pre-posting.

üü° MEDIUM: Fraud Risk

Risk: Users game referral system (fake accounts, referral farms)

Impact: Budget waste, skewed metrics

Mitigation: Implement fraud detection: (1) Both referrer and referee must fund account, (2) Referee must make 3 transactions, (3) Device fingerprinting to detect duplicates, (4) Manual review of Leyenda tier users.

üü¢ LOW: Email Deliverability

Risk: Emails land in spam, open rates <10%

Impact: Campaign underperforms by 20%

Mitigation: Warm up sending domain gradually (start 1K emails/day, ramp to 50K over 2 weeks). Use SendGrid or Mailgun. Monitor sender reputation weekly.

üü¢ LOW: Survey Fatigue

Risk: Over-surveying customers reduces future response rates

Impact: Harder to gather insights for future campaigns

Mitigation: Limit to 1 survey per customer per quarter. Incentivize with $5 Amazon gift card. Keep surveys <3 minutes.

üü¢ LOW: Competitive Response

Risk: Competitors (Chime, Dave) copy referral program

Impact: Reduced differentiation

Mitigation: Focus on cultural differentiation (familia, community) not just economics. Build moat through authentic relationships, not just bonuses.

Crisis Playbook: If Things Go Wrong

Scenario 1: Campaign Underperforms (Week 4 Check)

Red Flag: Tu Primera Semana completion rate <50% (target: 70%)

Actions:

  • Day 1: Pull detailed funnel analysis - where are users dropping off?
  • Day 2: Interview 10 users who didn't complete - what was confusing?
  • Day 3: A/B test simplified messaging: "Fund $10, get $3 instantly" vs current
  • Day 4: Increase Day 1 bonus from $3 to $5 (test with 500 users)
  • Day 5: Add SMS reminder in addition to email (test with 500 users)
  • Week 2: Implement winning variant, monitor for 7 more days
  • Week 3: If still <60%, pause campaign and redesign

Scenario 2: Referral Fraud Detected

Red Flag: 15% of referrals flagged as suspicious (same IP, device fingerprint)

Actions:

  • Immediate: Freeze payouts for all flagged accounts
  • Day 1: Manual review of top 20 referrers (Leyenda tier)
  • Day 2: Implement stricter requirements: referee must make 5 transactions (up from 3)
  • Day 3: Add email verification + phone verification for both parties
  • Day 4: Extend payout window from 7 days to 30 days (gives time to detect fraud)
  • Week 2: Partner with fraud detection service (Sift, Forter) if fraud >20%

Scenario 3: Influencer Creates Controversial Content

Red Flag: Partnered influencer posts politically divisive content, customers complaining

Actions:

  • Hour 1: Immediately pause all scheduled posts from that influencer
  • Hour 2: Reach out privately: "We're receiving customer feedback, need to discuss"
  • Hour 4: If influencer doesn't remove content, invoke contract termination clause
  • Day 1: Public statement: "We partner with diverse voices but don't endorse all views"
  • Day 2: Vet remaining influencers, add social listening alerts
  • Week 1: Update influencer guidelines: no political, religious, or divisive content
